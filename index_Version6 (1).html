Definition ‚Äî What is SDG 6?

SDG 6 aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. It covers safe drinking water, sanitation, hygiene, water quality, water-use efficiency, integrated water resources management, and transboundary cooperation.

Causes ‚Äî Why do water problems happen?

Common causes include pollution (industrial, agricultural), over-abstraction of groundwater, poor sanitation, lack of infrastructure, and climate change impacts such as droughts and floods.

Causes & indicators

  • Agricultural runoff (nitrates, pesticides)
  • Inadequate sanitation and sewage management
  • Excessive water extraction for irrigation
  • Climate-driven extremes (droughts/floods)

Interactive: click a cause for a short explanation

Effects ‚Äî What happens when water systems fail?

Effects include increased disease, food insecurity, lost livelihoods, ecosystem damage, and social conflict. Water problems disproportionately affect children, women and marginalized communities.

Effects snapshot

  • Health: waterborne diseases (diarrhea, cholera)
  • Economy: lost work days, agricultural losses
  • Environment: biodiversity loss and habitat degradation
